Rationale — Excerpt

This rationale unpacks the choices made for a visual translation of Angie Thomas’s novel. It argues that photography, typographic posters, and an interactive timeline together make visible the tensions of code-switching, public visibility, and communal resilience experienced by the protagonist. The final written rationale will include at least five direct quotations from the novel—anchoring images to lines that inspired composition—and at least two scholarly sources that situate the text within critical race theory and YA coming-of-age studies.

Visually, the website foregrounds Starr’s voice by placing typographic work alongside portraiture. The timeline pairs emotional moments with photographs to show how resilience grows through action and community. The written component will interlink these choices with textual analysis to meet the project’s rubric.

Planned quotes (examples to include): direct lines from the novel that emphasize voice, fear, and hope. (These will be cited in the final 1,200-word rationale.)
